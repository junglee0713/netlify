In ecology, the Shannon diversity of a community is defined by \[H=-\sum_{i}^R p_i\log p_i,\] where \(R\) is the number of distinct species and \(p_i\) is the proportion of species \(i\). The Shannon diversity measures richness (i.e. the number of distinct species in a community) and evenness (i.e. how evenly the proportions of species are spread).
